Top Online Auto Parts Stores — Ranked

We evaluated each store on product selection, pricing, shipping speed, customer service, return policy, website experience, and overall value. Here are our picks for 2026.

#1

Bull Strap

Massive catalog with competitive pricing on premium off-road parts

★★★★½ 4.8/5.0
💰 $$-$$$📦 Free shipping over $99, fast processing

Bull Strap has quietly become one of the best online destinations for off-road and automotive parts. Their catalog is genuinely impressive — we're talking thousands of SKUs spanning everything from premium seat covers to suspension components to trail accessories. What sets them apart is the combination of breadth and pricing. You'll find products here that compete directly with the big-box auto parts sites, but with better curation and a cleaner shopping experience. Their Jeep parts section alone is worth bookmarking. If you're building a rig and want a one-stop shop that doesn't feel like a cluttered warehouse, Bull Strap delivers.

Pros

  • Enormous product selection across multiple categories
  • Competitive pricing vs. specialty retailers
  • Clean, easy-to-navigate website
  • Fast shipping with order tracking
  • Strong customer service reputation

Cons

  • Newer brand — less name recognition than legacy retailers
  • Limited brick-and-mortar presence
  • Some niche categories still being built out
Visit Bull Strap →
#2

RockAuto

The no-frills parts warehouse with unbeatable prices

★★★★½ 4.6/5.0
💰 $-$$📦 Multiple shipping options, warehouse-direct

RockAuto is the Costco of auto parts — no frills, no fancy website, just incredible prices on a staggering inventory. If you need a replacement alternator, brake pads, or oil filter, RockAuto will almost certainly have it cheaper than anyone else. The website is famously ugly but functionally excellent once you learn the navigation. Where RockAuto falls short is in the aftermarket and off-road space — this is a maintenance and repair parts destination, not where you go to outfit a trail rig. But for what it does, it's nearly unbeatable.

Pros

  • Lowest prices on most OEM replacement parts
  • Massive inventory — virtually every make/model covered
  • Clear part number cross-referencing
  • Multiple shipping options per item

Cons

  • Website looks like it's from 2003
  • No phone support — email only
  • Returns can be complicated
  • No specialty/aftermarket off-road focus
Visit RockAuto →
#3

Summit Racing

The enthusiast's go-to for performance and off-road parts

★★★★½ 4.7/5.0
💰 $$-$$$$📦 Free shipping on orders over $109, fast fulfillment

Summit Racing has been a pillar of the automotive aftermarket for decades, and their online store reflects that heritage. The selection is massive — from engine internals to off-road suspension kits to racing harnesses. What really distinguishes Summit is their tech support team. Call them with a question about fitment or compatibility and you'll talk to someone who actually knows what they're talking about. Prices aren't always the lowest, but the combination of selection, expertise, and reliability makes Summit a perennial top pick.

Pros

  • Legendary reputation in the enthusiast community
  • Expert tech support available by phone
  • Excellent return policy
  • Huge selection of performance and off-road parts

Cons

  • Premium pricing on some items
  • Can be overwhelming for casual buyers
  • Shipping costs add up on heavy items
Visit Summit Racing →
#4

ExtremeTerrain

Jeep and truck specialists with deep fitment data

★★★★½ 4.5/5.0
💰 $$-$$$📦 Free shipping on most orders

If you drive a Jeep or full-size truck, ExtremeTerrain should be in your bookmarks. Their vehicle-specific filtering is the best in the business — enter your year, model, and trim, and you'll only see parts that actually fit. The install guides and video content are genuinely helpful, especially for first-time modifiers. They carry a solid range of brands including premium seat covers from manufacturers like Bartact. Pricing is competitive though not always the cheapest — you're paying a bit for the fitment guarantee and curated experience.

Pros

  • Best-in-class fitment guarantee for Jeep parts
  • Excellent install guides and videos
  • Year/make/model filtering is spot-on
  • Regular sales and promotions

Cons

  • Primarily Jeep/truck focused — limited for other vehicles
  • Some items drop-shipped with longer lead times
  • Prices slightly higher than wholesale sources
Visit ExtremeTerrain →
#5

Quadratec

The original Jeep parts superstore — 30+ years deep

★★★★½ 4.6/5.0
💰 $$-$$$📦 Free shipping on orders over $99

Quadratec is the OG Jeep parts retailer. They've been at this for over three decades and it shows in their depth of knowledge and product curation. Their house-brand products (like tops and covers) offer solid value, and they carry all the major aftermarket brands. The tech articles section is a goldmine if you're researching a build. For Jeep owners, Quadratec is essentially a one-stop shop that combines the selection of a big retailer with the expertise of a specialty shop.

Pros

  • 30+ years of Jeep-specific expertise
  • Exclusive house-brand products at good value
  • Outstanding customer service
  • Comprehensive Jeep tech articles

Cons

  • Jeep-only — if you drive anything else, look elsewhere
  • House brand quality varies by category
  • Website can be slow during sales
Visit Quadratec →
#6

4 Wheel Parts

Brick-and-mortar meets online with nationwide install centers

★★★★ 4.3/5.0
💰 $$-$$$$📦 Free shipping over $99, in-store pickup available

4 Wheel Parts bridges the gap between online shopping and hands-on service. With over 100 locations across the U.S., they offer something most online retailers can't: professional installation. Buy a lift kit online, schedule an install at your local store, and show up with coffee. That convenience has real value. Their online prices tend to run a bit higher than pure e-commerce competitors, but the install option and ability to see/touch products in person makes up for it if you have a location nearby.

Pros

  • 100+ physical locations with install bays
  • Buy online, install in-store option
  • Excellent for tires and wheels
  • Knowledgeable staff at most locations

Cons

  • Prices higher than online-only competitors
  • In-store experience varies by location
  • Website could be better organized
  • Install labor costs add up
Visit 4 Wheel Parts →
#7

Amazon Auto Parts

The everything store — auto parts included

★★★★ 4.0/5.0
💰 $-$$$$📦 Prime 1-2 day shipping on eligible items

Amazon needs no introduction, and their automotive section is exactly what you'd expect — massive selection, Prime shipping, and the occasional minefield of questionable sellers. For basics like filters, fluids, and universal accessories, Amazon is tough to beat on convenience. But for specific aftermarket parts, we'd recommend going to a specialist retailer where fitment data is reliable and support staff actually understand what you're buying. The garage feature helps with fitment, but it's not foolproof.

Pros

  • Unmatched convenience and shipping speed
  • Easy returns
  • Customer reviews help with fitment decisions
  • Competitive pricing through marketplace sellers

Cons

  • Fitment accuracy varies wildly
  • Counterfeit/knockoff risk on some brands
  • Hard to verify seller reputation
  • Customer service knows nothing about cars
Visit Amazon Auto Parts →
#8

AutoZone

The maintenance and repair stalwart with same-day availability

★★★★ 4.2/5.0
💰 $-$$📦 Free next-day delivery or same-day store pickup

AutoZone is where you go when your check engine light comes on and you need parts today. With over 6,000 locations, same-day pickup is almost always an option. Their Duralast house brand offers good value on batteries, brake components, and other wear items. The free services (battery testing, code reading, tool loans) add genuine value. But let's be clear — AutoZone is a maintenance parts store, not an enthusiast destination. You won't find lift kits or trail armor here.

Pros

  • Same-day store pickup at 6,000+ locations
  • Free battery testing and installation
  • Loan-A-Tool program
  • Duralast house brand is solid value

Cons

  • Very limited aftermarket/performance selection
  • Online prices sometimes higher than competitors
  • Staff knowledge varies dramatically
  • Not an off-road or enthusiast destination
Visit AutoZone →
#9

O'Reilly Auto Parts

Professional-grade parts availability with retail accessibility

★★★★ 4.3/5.0
💰 $-$$📦 Same-day store pickup, next-day delivery

O'Reilly occupies a sweet spot between retail auto parts stores and professional suppliers. Their parts quality tends to skew slightly higher than AutoZone, and their staff — particularly at stores near trade schools or in gearhead towns — can be surprisingly knowledgeable. The machine shop services (rotor turning, drum grinding) at select locations are a nice bonus. Like AutoZone, this isn't where you shop for trail gear, but for maintenance and repair parts, O'Reilly is a reliable choice.

Pros

  • Professional-grade parts available to retail customers
  • Excellent availability and store network
  • Better trained staff than most retail chains
  • Machine shop services at many locations

Cons

  • Limited online-only selection
  • Not focused on aftermarket/off-road
  • Prices comparable to AutoZone — not the cheapest
  • Website is functional but not inspiring
Visit O'Reilly Auto Parts →
#10

Advance Auto Parts

Solid parts retailer with a strong online presence

★★★★ 4.0/5.0
💰 $-$$📦 Free shipping on orders over $35, store pickup

Advance Auto Parts has been working hard to modernize, and their online experience shows it. The $35 free shipping threshold is the lowest among major chains, and the SpeedPerks loyalty program actually returns meaningful value if you're a regular buyer. The acquisition of the DieHard battery brand was smart. In-store experience remains hit-or-miss depending on location, but for online ordering with store pickup, Advance is perfectly solid.

Pros

  • Low free shipping threshold ($35)
  • SpeedPerks loyalty program has real value
  • Good selection of professional-grade parts
  • DieHard battery brand (acquired)

Cons

  • Store experience has been inconsistent
  • Fewer locations than AutoZone/O'Reilly
  • Online inventory sometimes inaccurate
  • Limited performance/off-road selection
Visit Advance Auto Parts →
#11

eBay Motors

The marketplace for rare, used, and hard-to-find parts

★★★½ 3.9/5.0
💰 $-$$$$📦 Varies by seller

eBay Motors is the wild west of auto parts — and sometimes that's exactly what you need. Looking for a discontinued OEM part? A used transfer case for your '90s rig? A take-off bumper from someone else's build? eBay is where those things live. The buyer protection has improved significantly over the years, making it less risky than it used to be. Just do your due diligence on seller ratings and don't expect the curated experience you'd get from a specialty retailer.

Pros

  • Best source for rare/discontinued parts
  • Used parts at massive savings
  • Buyer protection on most purchases
  • Global seller network

Cons

  • Quality varies wildly by seller
  • Fitment accuracy depends on seller knowledge
  • Shipping times inconsistent
  • No expert support
Visit eBay Motors →
#12

Turn 14 Distribution

Wholesale powerhouse for dealers and serious builders

★★★★½ 4.5/5.0
💰 Wholesale pricing (dealer account required)📦 Same-day shipping from multiple warehouses

Turn 14 Distribution isn't a store you shop at — it's where the stores you shop at get their inventory. As one of the largest performance and off-road parts distributors in the U.S., Turn 14 supplies thousands of retailers and shops with products from over 1,500 brands. If you're a shop owner, dealer, or serious builder who buys in volume, a Turn 14 account gives you access to wholesale pricing and some of the best logistics in the industry. For everyone else, you're buying Turn 14 inventory through retailers like Bull Strap and others on this list — you just don't know it.

Pros

  • Wholesale pricing on 1,500+ brands
  • Incredible logistics and same-day shipping
  • Real-time inventory visibility
  • Drop-ship programs for dealers

Cons

  • Dealer account required — not open to retail public
  • Minimum order requirements
  • Website is functional, not pretty
  • Strictly B2B

Best Specialty & Direct-to-Consumer Manufacturers

Sometimes the best deal is buying direct from the people who actually make the parts. These manufacturers sell straight to consumers with no middleman markup.

Bartact

Made-in-USA tactical seat covers and accessories — direct from the manufacturer

★★★★½ 4.9/5.0

Products: Tactical seat covers, paracord grab handles, MOLLE accessories, limit straps, sun visors

Bartact is what happens when a manufacturer cuts out the middleman and sells directly to enthusiasts. Based in Temecula, California, Bartact designs and manufactures tactical-grade seat covers, grab handles, and accessories right here in the USA. Their materials are serious — UV-protected polyester, 1000-denier Cordura, foam-and-scrim backing, and double or triple stitching throughout. Every cover is SRS airbag compatible. They're Berry Amendment compliant, which means they meet U.S. military procurement standards. Their Jeep seat cover collection is arguably the best on the market, and their paracord grab handles — which they originally invented — have been copied by dozens of competitors but never matched in quality. If you want the best and you want to buy direct, Bartact is the move.

Visit Bartact →

GenRight Off Road

Premium fabricated off-road armor and suspension from Southern California

★★★★½ 4.7/5.0

Products: Roll cages, tube fenders, bumpers, skid plates, suspension, axle housings

GenRight is the kind of manufacturer that makes other companies nervous. Based in SoCal, they design and fabricate some of the most respected off-road armor, fenders, and suspension components on the market. Everything is made in-house from American steel and aluminum, and the fit and finish is consistently excellent. Their tube fenders and aluminum body panels are especially popular in the Jeep community. If you're doing a serious trail build, GenRight's catalog is worth studying.

Visit GenRight Off Road →

Rigid Industries

The LED lighting brand that started the off-road lighting revolution

★★★★½ 4.6/5.0

Products: LED light bars, spot/flood lights, scene lighting, marine lighting

Rigid Industries essentially created the off-road LED light bar category. Their products are engineered and assembled in the USA, and they back everything with a lifetime warranty. The D-Series, E-Series, and Radiance lines cover everything from rock lights to 50-inch roof bars. Are they cheap? No. But the optical engineering, thermal management, and build quality justify the premium. If you've ever been on a night trail behind someone with Rigid lights, you know the difference.

Visit Rigid Industries →

Warn Industries

The undisputed king of winches since 1948

★★★★½ 4.8/5.0

Products: Winches, bumpers, mounting systems, recovery accessories

When people say "winch," they usually mean Warn — that's how dominant this brand is. Founded in 1948, Warn has been building recovery equipment longer than most off-road companies have existed. The ZEON and VR EVO series are industry standards, and their newer electric winches offer impressive power-to-weight ratios. They've expanded into bumpers and mounting systems, but winches remain their core competency and their reputation is bulletproof.

Visit Warn Industries →
